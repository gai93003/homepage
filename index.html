Discover more about my city

Birmingham is a vibrant city known for its extensive canal network, boasting more miles of canals than Venice. It has a youthful population, with nearly 40% under the age of 25, and is famous for its culinary innovation, being the birthplace of the Balti curry. The city is a cultural hub, home to the largest public library in Europe and a significant collection of Pre-Raphaelite art. Birmingham is also one of the greenest cities in the UK, with over 8,000 acres of parks. Historically, it was a manufacturing powerhouse during the Industrial Revolution, earning the nickname “City of a Thousand Trades.” The city has a rich musical heritage, being the birthplace of heavy metal, and has notable sporting history, including the founding of the Football League and the first ODEON cinema.
